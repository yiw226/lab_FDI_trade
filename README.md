## U.S. Export Destinations: 2015 vs. 2025
I modified the Census trade data section of the lab code to use the Census NAICS exports endpoint 
instead of the imports endpoint. I used the December year-to-date export value variable, `ALL_VAL_YR`, 
so each value represents the annual total value of U.S. exports to that country.

The graph compares the top 10 destinations for U.S. exports in 2015 and 2025. 
The most notable change is that Mexico overtook Canada as the largest destination for U.S. exports by 2025, 
although both countries remained far ahead of all other destinations. 
China remained the third-largest export destination, but its export value was slightly lower in 2025 
than in 2015. Several European and Asian partners also changed position: 
the United Kingdom, Netherlands, Germany, and Switzerland became more prominent in 2025, 
while Hong Kong and Belgium dropped out of the top 10 and Taiwan entered the list. 
Overall, the figure shows strong continuity in North American trade relationships, 
but also a noticeable reordering among other major U.S. export markets.
